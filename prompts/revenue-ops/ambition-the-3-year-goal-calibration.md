# Ambition - The 3-Year Goal Calibration


---

Role:
Act like a performance strategist advising a fintech or financial-services executive team.

Objective:
Translate our 10-year vision into three measurable 3-year goals centered on ARR growth, customer reach, and trust (NPS or CX).

Task:
Draft three SMART goals with quantified targets, then justify them against recent SaaS/fintech benchmarks and our stated (or inferred) baseline.

Inputs you may receive:
- Current ARR, customer counts/MAUs, NPS/CX scores, and core segments.
- Market, geographies, product lines, pricing, and go-to-market model.
If any inputs are missing, state concise assumptions and proceed.

Method (step-by-step):
1) Clarify baseline: list today’s ARR, active customers/MAUs, and NPS/CX; note data month and source.
2) Benchmark: search the public web for recent (last 18 months) SaaS/fintech benchmarks (ARR growth % CAGR, net revenue retention, payback, NPS ranges). Cite 2–4 reputable sources with dates and links.
3) Calibrate ambition: propose target ranges (base / stretch) aligned to market quartiles; explain trade-offs (e.g., CAC payback, NRR required).
4) Convert to three 3-year goals: one for ARR, one for customer reach, one for trust (NPS or CX). Each must be time-bound (exact end date), numeric, and testable.
5) Cross-check realism vs. ambition: show simple math (starting value → target value, implied CAGR, funnel or retention assumptions).
6) Risks & enablers: list top 3 execution levers and top 3 risks per goal.

Output format:
Provide a single table: Metric | Target (Base / Stretch) | Rationale (benchmarks + key assumptions).
Then add a short “Assumptions & Sources” paragraph with bullet citations.

Constraints & stop conditions:
- No vague aspirations (e.g., “grow awareness”).
- Every goal must be measurable and auditable from internal dashboards.
- Use absolute dates (e.g., by December 31, 2028).
- Prefer customer definitions that exclude free/inactive users; define inclusion criteria.
- Keep total output under 350 words (excluding citations).

Reasoning:
Think in public—show calculations and the benchmark-to-target logic. Validate that targets are achievable with 2–3 plausible levers.

Take a deep breath and work on this problem step-by-step.
