# Personas


---

Prompt 1 — Define Who You Serve
Role – You are a fintech or financial-services product leader clarifying your audience.
Task – Identify and describe the 3 most valuable customer personas your business serves.
Context – Use both quantitative inputs (market sizing, conversion data, usage analytics) and qualitative sources (interviews, reviews, customer feedback). Reference the Dot Connector Persona Framework fields: Market Sizing, Demographics, Work Tensions, and Drivers to Hire/Buy/Subscribe.
Output Format – Table with persona names, segment size (TAM/SAM/SOM), and key differentiating traits.
Reasoning Steps – Segment → Prioritize → Name → Validate.
Constraints & Stop Conditions – Limit to three personas. Exclude speculative or unvalidated assumptions. Focus on data-supported insights only.

Prompt 2 — Diagnose Their Deepest Tensions
Role – You are a strategist uncovering the emotional and operational tensions shaping your customers’ decisions.
Task – Analyze what keeps each persona up at night and the contradictions in their daily work (e.g., growth vs. control, speed vs. safety).
Context – Use persona data, customer feedback, or qualitative research. Draw from the “Work Tensions” and “What Keeps Them Up at Night” sections of the framework.
Output Format – Narrative summary (2–3 sentences per persona) paired with a one-line tension statement.
Reasoning Steps – Identify → Interpret → Synthesize → Validate with data or quotes.
Constraints & Stop Conditions – Stay grounded in customer truth. No invented pain points or assumptions without evidence.

Prompt 3 — Design the Magnetic Offer
Role – You are a marketing strategist designing offers that resonate emotionally and logically.
Task – Apply a “Magnetic Offer Design” model (Pain → Dream → Blockers → Upgrade → Magnetic Angle) for your top persona.
Context – Combine your internal data with insights from surveys, reviews, or NPS responses.
Output Format – Table or structured paragraph summarizing each stage of the model.
Reasoning Steps – Map the current state → Define the dream → Identify blockers → Frame the upgrade → Craft the magnetic angle.
Constraints & Stop Conditions – Limit to one primary persona per run. Keep each element under 50 words for clarity and focus.

Prompt 4 — Translate Persona Insight Into Product Strategy
Role – You are a product leader aligning personas with roadmap decisions.
Task – Use persona insights to define 3 high-impact product or feature priorities that address their unmet needs.
Context – Reference customer pain points, behavioral analytics, and qualitative interviews. Tie each to measurable business outcomes (revenue growth, retention, NPS).
Output Format – List of three product priorities with rationale and KPI alignment.
Reasoning Steps – Map persona need → Link to feature concept → Align to business outcome.
Constraints & Stop Conditions – Limit to 3 priorities. Avoid hypothetical “nice-to-haves.” Focus on clarity, not creativity.

Prompt 5 — Validate and Refresh Personas Quarterly
Role – You are a growth operator maintaining persona relevance over time.
Task – Audit each existing persona to ensure data freshness, behavioral accuracy, and strategic alignment.
Context – Review your CRM, analytics, and feedback data. Apply the Dot Connector “Validate & Control” step from the framework.
Output Format – Table with persona name, last updated date, key change observed, and next signal to watch.
Reasoning Steps – Assess → Compare → Update → Signal next review.
Constraints & Stop Conditions – Limit to existing personas only. End when all personas have been updated or retired.
