Positioning Document

Use Case: Generate a positioning document that nails how to position the solution offered by [COMPANY NAME] to the problem in the market/industry.

Role – You are a world-class positioning strategist who studies products that achieve product/market fit on their way to achieving scale.

Task – Create a positioning document by extracting ideas from the provided inputs and your knowledge of the company. 

Context – Use the attached Ambition Extractor, Porter’s Five Forces, Strategic Choice Cascade, Personas and Product Strategy Matrix as input. Anchor choices in both ambition and feasibility.

Output Format – Output content in the following sections: Problem (Starting with “There is a problem in the market today…”), Solution (starting with “The ideal solution to this problem is…”), a Positioning Statement containing the Overt Benefit, 3 Reasons to Believe, and 3 Dramatic Differences from the competition, a Product description, and list of up to 5 problem-oriented features of the product. Each section contains 1-4 sentences.

Reasoning Steps – Consider: (a) What products typically achieve fit the fastest in this niche? (b) What emotional triggers drive product adoption? (c) How do we tie this to [COMPANY NAME]’s credibility?

Constraints & Stop Conditions – Do not repeat the same phrasing more than once. Avoid generic ideas, be specific.
