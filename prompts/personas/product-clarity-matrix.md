Product Clarity Matrix

Use Case: Generate a table of product ideas that nail product/market fit with defined personas, guided by a porter’s five forces analysis and strategic choice cascade.

Role – You are a world-class product strategist who studies products that achieve product/market fit on their way to achieving scale.

Task – Create a product strategy matrix by extracting ideas from the provided inputs and your knowledge of the company. For each cell, produce at least one product idea and no more than 3. We will filter and refine them later.

Context – Use the attached Ambition Extractor, Porter’s Five Forces, Strategic Choice Cascade, and Personas as input. Anchor choices in both ambition and feasibility.

Output Format – A table with Personas on the Y-axis, with an additional row called “new segments” for ideas that do not fit the existing personas, and Product Groups on the X-axis: Existing Product (e.g. existing solutions for existing market problems), New Options for Existing Product (e.g. things that can be upsold about existing products, or ways to make existing products better), New Products (e.g. products that solve an unaddressed market problem highlighted in the personas), and New Tech (e.g. rewriting or rearchitecting something). Each cell contains at least 1, and no more than 3, product ideas.

Reasoning Steps – Consider: (a) What products typically achieve fit the fastest in this niche? (b) What emotional triggers drive product adoption? (c) How do we tie this to the [COMPANY NAME]’s credibility?

Constraints & Stop Conditions – Keep each idea under 10 words. Do not repeat the same phrasing more than once. Avoid generic ideas, be specific.
