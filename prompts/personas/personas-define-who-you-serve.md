# Personas - Define Who You Serve


---

Role:
Act like a fintech/financial-services product leader and senior customer-research analyst.

Objective:
Identify and describe the 3 most valuable customer personas the business serves, grounded in verified quantitative and qualitative evidence for a browser-based ChatGPT session.

Task:
Produce exactly three data-supported personas that map to the Dot Connector Persona Framework fields and quantify opportunity (TAM/SAM/SOM), conversion, usage, and value.

Inputs you may use:
- Quant: market sizing reports, funnel/conversion data, activation/retention/usage analytics, ARPU/LTV, cohort analyses.
- Qual: interview notes, reviews, customer-support logs, NPS/CSAT verbatims, sales notes.

Method (Reasoning Steps = Segment → Prioritize → Name → Validate):
1) Segment: Cluster users by job-to-be-done, behaviors, willingness-to-pay, firmographics/demographics. Prefer observed data to opinions.
2) Prioritize: Score segments by impact = (SOM × LTV × Retention) ÷ (CAC × Time-to-Value). Break ties with growth momentum and strategic fit.
3) Name: Create memorable, role-anchored names tied to core job-to-be-done (avoid clichés).
4) Validate: For each persona, list 2–4 concrete evidence points (metrics, quotes, sources). If data is missing, mark as “Insufficient evidence” and specify the exact data required.

Dot Connector Persona Framework fields to fill per persona:
- Market Sizing (TAM/SAM/SOM with sources and assumptions)
- Demographics (or firmographics for B2B)
- Work Tensions (conflicts, constraints, trade-offs)
- What Keeps Them Up at Night (top anxieties, risks)
- Drivers to Hire/Buy/Subscribe (triggers, value props, must-haves)

Constraints & Stop Conditions:
- Limit to three personas.
- Exclude speculative or unvalidated assumptions. Every claim must reference a metric, a source, or a direct quote.
- If browsing is enabled, cite sources for market sizing; if not, request the minimal dataset to compute TAM/SAM/SOM and proceed only when provided.

Output Format (one table, exactly three rows):
Columns: Persona Name | Segment Size (TAM/SAM/SOM) | Demographics | Work Tensions | What Keeps Them Up at Night | Drivers to Hire/Buy/Subscribe | Key Differentiating Traits | Evidence (metrics/quotes/sources).

Self-check before finalizing:
- Evidence present for each cell? 
- Numbers reconcile (SOM ≤ SAM ≤ TAM)? 
- Prioritization math shown or referenced? 
- Exactly three personas?

Take a deep breath and work on this problem step-by-step.
