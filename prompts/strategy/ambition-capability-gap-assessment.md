# Ambition - Capability Gap Assessment


---

Role:
Act like an organizational strategist and operating-model architect.

Objective:
Identify 1–2 new organizational capabilities the company must build in the next 3 years to hit its goals, focusing on scalable, skill-based levers—not tools or headcount vanity.

Inputs you may receive (use if provided; otherwise infer from context): 3-year goals, current capabilities, org structure, culture signals, resource constraints, customer insights, performance data.

Process (follow step-by-step, reason privately, then produce only the requested outputs):
1) Extract or infer the company’s 3-year outcomes and value-creation thesis.
2) Inventory current capabilities across product innovation, data analytics, brand storytelling, and talent development (people, process, governance, and metrics).
3) Assess cultural readiness and existing resources (skills depth, leadership sponsorship, operating cadence, data/data quality, budgets).
4) Map gaps to goals; identify the few capabilities most causally linked to goal attainment.
5) Propose 1–2 net-new capabilities (skill-based) with: definition, why-now, scope (people/process/tools/governance), starter KPIs, and minimal viable pilot.
6) Pressure-test against constraints: exclude technology wishlists, avoid vanity hires; ensure cross-functional scalability and compounding learning effects.
7) Finalize outputs exactly per the format below.

Output Format (strict):
A) Short narrative summary (≤150 words) that states the 1–2 capabilities, the problem they solve, expected business impact in 3 years, and the first 90-day pilot focus.
B) A 2-column markdown table labeled: Current Capability | Needed Capability. Each row maps a specific current state to a precise new capability name.

Constraints & Stop Conditions:
- No tool lists, platforms, or brand-name tech.
- No role inflation; focus on upskilling, rituals, reusable playbooks, and operating mechanisms.
- Max 2 capabilities; both must be teachable, measurable, and scalable across teams.
- Prefer approaches that create durable learning loops (cadence, templates, shared datasets).

Quality & Self-Check before answering:
- Word count of narrative ≤150.
- Table present with exactly two columns and 2–4 rows.
- Clear causal link to 3-year goals.
- Culturally feasible within stated resources.

Take a deep breath and work on this problem step-by-step.
