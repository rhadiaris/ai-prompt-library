# Competition


---

Prompt 1 — Competitive Clarity Extractor
Role: You are a fintech strategy consultant skilled in diagnosing competitive pressure using Porter’s Five Forces and DMAIC.
Task: Identify the top two competitive pressures facing my company right now.
Context: Use the following inputs — company type, target customer, region, and key products. Reference benchmark data where available.
Output Format: Table showing each force, FII score (1–5), one-sentence rationale, and direction (↑ ↓ →).
Reasoning Steps: Compare historical shifts, entrant growth, and substitute adoption; justify FII scores logically.
Constraints & Stop Conditions: Limit to one page; exclude textbook definitions or general theory.

Prompt 2 — Rivalry Intelligence Builder
Role: You are a market-intelligence analyst.
Task: Map our top five direct competitors and summarize their current positioning versus ours.
Context: Focus on fintechs in [market/region]. Consider pricing, UX, distribution, and brand strength.
Output Format: Competitive matrix with columns for differentiators, weaknesses, and estimated FII impact.
Reasoning Steps: Rank each competitor by how much they increase rivalry pressure; infer trends.
Constraints & Stop Conditions: No fluff or marketing jargon. Keep output actionable.

Prompt 3 — Substitute Signal Detector
Role: You are a customer-behavior strategist.
Task: Identify alternative products or behaviors customers use to solve the same job-to-be-done outside our ecosystem.
Context: Focus on the last 12 months of market change in [segment].
Output Format: Bullet list of substitutes with adoption data, threat rating (1–5), and short narrative on why they appeal.
Reasoning Steps: Connect behavior shifts to friction in our current offering; infer where innovation is needed.
Constraints & Stop Conditions: Ignore low-adoption experiments; focus only on live or scaling substitutes.

Prompt 4 — Buyer Power Audit
Role: You are a customer experience researcher.
Task: Assess the degree of buyer power in our market and identify 3 levers to reduce defection risk.
Context: Use churn data, open banking developments, and customer feedback themes.
Output Format: Three-part structure — (1) Power Source, (2) Impact, (3) Recommended Action.
Reasoning Steps: Evaluate emotional, economic, and friction-based loyalty drivers.
Constraints & Stop Conditions: Keep under 400 words; avoid sentiment-only commentary.

Prompt 5 — Competitive Control Dashboard
Role: You are a Chief Strategy Officer designing a monitoring system.
Task: Build a quarterly Competitive Clarity Dashboard template.
Context: Integrate KPIs for each force: e.g., market share change (Rivalry), entrant count (Entrants), substitute adoption rate (Substitutes), churn rate (Buyers), vendor dependency index (Suppliers).
Output Format: Markdown table with metrics, owners, update frequency, and data sources.
Reasoning Steps: Justify why each metric signals pressure and how it connects to strategic action.
Constraints & Stop Conditions: One-page dashboard summary. Prioritize clarity and repeatability over aesthetics.
