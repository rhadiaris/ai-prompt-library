# Whiteboard Creation


---

Generate a single image of a physical, hand-drawn infographic on a large whiteboard or notebook page.

Crucial Style Instructions (Read First):

Medium: The image must look like a photograph of a real whiteboard or large paper notepad.

Texture: All elements must look created by hand using colored marker pens (black, blue, red, green) and highlighters (yellow/orange). 

Lines should be slightly imperfect, wobbly, and have the texture of ink on a surface.

No Digital Fonts: All text, headings, and bullet points must appear handwritten or hand-printed in marker pen.

Layout: Structure the 1080x1350 image as follows:
Use multi-colored markers for emphasis. Keep text large and legible. Make everything look hand-drawn with slight imperfections. Make it look like a photograph of an actual notebook page.
Always include the handwritten text “Follow Regis Hadiaris Dot Connector for more helpful content | Repost ♻️” at the bottom of the image, in the same hand-drawn marker style.

## Visual Style Guidelines

### Typography Hierarchy

- **Slide titles/labels:** 18pt, all caps, letter-spaced
- **Main stat/number:** 120–200pt, bold
- **Supporting text:** 24–32pt, regular weight
- **Bottom insights:** 28pt, medium weight, italics optional

### Iconography

- Minimal, functional icons only
- Use sparingly (warning symbol, arrows, simple shapes)
- No decorative elements that don’t serve data

### White Space

- Each slide should breathe
- Don’t fill every corner
- Let the data be the hero

### Data Visualization Principles

- Simple > complex
- Show relationships, not just numbers
- Every visual element should answer: “So what?”
- If you can say it in text, don’t illustrate it

## Design Handoff Notes for Designer

**What matters most:**

1. **Readability on mobile** - LinkedIn users scroll on phones. Type must be legible at 375px width.
1. **Quotability** - Every slide should work as a standalone screenshot.
1. **Data emphasis** - Numbers should dominate. Let statistics do the talking.
1. **Brand consistency** - This should look like the Dot Connector system: clear, confident, spare.

**What to avoid:**

- Gradient backgrounds
- Drop shadows
- Decorative illustrations
- Stock photo people
- Generic business icons (lightbulbs, rockets, graphs)
- Busy patterns or textures

**Reference style:**
Think: *Financial Times data viz meets Stripe’s design system*

- Clean
- Confident
- Data-forward
- Professional without being corporate
